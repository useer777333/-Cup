Our Story

Born in the Himalayas,
Crafted for the World

Cup was born from a simple observation in the bustling tea houses of Kathmandu — thousands of disposable cups discarded every day, polluting our beautiful valleys and rivers.


We asked: what if you could just eat the cup? Our team of food scientists and bakers spent two years perfecting a cup made from high-quality Nepali flour — crispy, flavorful, and strong enough to hold piping hot tea for 40 to 45 minutes.


Today, Cup is more than a product — it's a movement. A movement toward zero-waste tea culture, rooted in the traditions and innovation of Nepal.

Our Journey

Milestones That
Shaped Cup

2022 — The Idea

A Problem Noticed

Our founders noticed the massive plastic cup waste in Kathmandu's tea stalls and envisioned an edible alternative.

2023 — R&D Phase

Perfecting the Recipe

After 200+ experiments, we cracked the formula for a cup that's both heat-resistant and delicious.

2024 — Launch

Cup Goes Live

First batch sold in Kathmandu. Went viral on social media. 10,000 cups sold in the first month.

2025 — Scaling Up

National Expansion

Expanded to 150+ cafés across Nepal. Launched new flavors, bulk orders, and began exploring international markets.
